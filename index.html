Ryan W. Pfeifle, Ph.D

I am a NASA Postdoctoral Program (NPP) Fellow at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland, and I work with Dr. Kim Weaver in the X-Ray Laboratory in the Astrophysics Division. I received my Ph.D in Physics from George Mason University in May 2022 under the direction of Prof. Shobita Satyapal in the Black Hole - Galaxy Connection Group . My work focuses on identifying heavily absorbed dual AGN and triple AGN in colliding galaxies and understanding the relationship between the AGN and the host galaxies. I primarily hunt for these systems using X-ray telescopes such as Chandra, XMM-Newton, and NuSTAR, but I also employ optical and near-infrared diagnostics in the identification and characterization of these elusive giants.

Galaxy Mergers and Supermassive Black Holes

Using state-of-the-art X-ray telescopes in orbit, I hunt for systems of merging galaxies in which there are pairs actively feeding supermassive black holes. These black holes, which reside at the centers of their respective galaxies and can reach masses of several hundred million to a few billion times the mass of the sun, are expected to consume vast quantities of gas and dust during the merger event. Due to this vast inflow of material from the galaxy to the black holes during the merger sequence, mergers may play a key role in the growth and evolution of these black holes over time...
